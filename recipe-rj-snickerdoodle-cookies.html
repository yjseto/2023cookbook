Snickerdoodle Cookies

Fun Fact: The snickerdoodle has been called many names, including "Cry Babies," "Jumbles" and "Plunkets." In the early 1900s, there was a popular folk character named Snickerdoodle who was the subject of many tall tales. It's claimed that the cookie might have been named after this fanciful folk hero.

Snickerdoodles are characterized by a cracked surface and can be either crisp or soft depending on the ingredients used. They are often referred to as "sugar cookies". However, traditional sugar cookies are often rolled in white sugar whereas snickerdoodles are rolled in a mixture of white sugar and cinnamon. Cream of tartar is added for its signature texture as another main difference.

Ingredients

  • 3 c. (360 g.) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp. cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt
  • 3/4 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 c. (2 sticks) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 c. (300 g.) granulated sugar, divided
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp. ground cinnamon

Instructions

Follow these four simple steps and you will have delicious cookies in no time!

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk flour, cream of tartar, salt, and baking soda until combined. In a large bowl, using a handheld mixer on medium-high speed, beat butter and 1 1/4 cups sugar until creamy. Add eggs, one at a time, beating to blend after each addition. Beat in vanilla. Add dry ingredients and beat on low speed until just combined. Cover and refrigerate until chilled, at least 1 hour or up to overnight.
  2. Preheat oven to 350°. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment. In a small bowl, whisk cinnamon and remaining 1/4 cup sugar.
  3. Using a medium cookie scoop (about 2 tablespoons), scoop dough and roll into balls. Toss balls in cinnamon sugar and arrange on prepared sheets, spacing 2" apart.
  4. Bake cookies until puffed and edges are just set, 10 to 12 minutes. Let cool on baking sheet 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and let cool completely.

Tips

If not prepared properly, snickerdoodles have a habit of going flat. Almost all snickerdoodle recipes call for baking soda rather than baking powder. Baking soda can lose its effectiveness if it sits on the shelf for too long. Always use fresh baking soda to ensure your snickerdoodles will maintain their trademark fluffiness. The cream of tartar should be fresh, as well.

Variations

Variations on the traditional snickerdoodle include the Chai snickerdoodle and the chocolate Chai snickerdoodle.

Chocolate Snickerdoodles

"This chocolate snickerdoodle recipe is similar to a basic snickerdoodle recipe but some of the flour is replaced with cocoa powder," explains recipe creator rhancox. With the combination of cocoa and cinnamon, the flavors are akin to Mexican hot chocolate.

Ultimate Maple Snickerdoodles

The duo of maple syrup and maple sugar make these snickerdoodles an autumnal treat. Reviewer andeesings says, "Love these! The maple flavor is nice but not overpowering."


Cranberry Snickerdoodles

Tangy dried cranberries are just what's needed to create these stand out snickerdoodles. The secret ingredient? A package of cream cheese, which is whipped into the dough to create irresistibly soft cookies.


Pumpkin Snickerdoodle

"These decadent cookies will melt in your mouth," shares recipe creator Jenn Harmon Jones. Puréed pumpkin and nutmeg infuse the dough, while a dash of allspice livens up the cinnamon-sugar topping.


Gluten-Free Snickerdoodles

No gluten? No problem! These snickerdoodles have all the sugar and spice you want, while using a gluten-free flour to make it safe for those with wheat sensitivities.


Eggnog Snickerdoodle

Combine all the goodness of snickerdoodles and your favorite holiday drink with these eggnog-inspired cookies. All you need are a dash each of rum and brandy extracts and some colorful sugar for rolling.


Chai Snickerdoodle

Recipe creator Melodie describes these cookies as "Fast, easy, tasty, and sweet with a kick of spice." Using pre-made chai concentrate means these are a cinch to make and each bite is infused with the heady flavors of chai spices.
